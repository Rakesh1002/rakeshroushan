# Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)

## Template Overview

Use this NDA before discussing business plans, showing code, or sharing confidential information with potential contractors, partners, or advisors.

## 1. DEFINITION OF CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION

### 1.1 "Confidential Information" means:
Any non-public information disclosed by one Party ("Disclosing Party") to the other Party ("Receiving Party"), including but not limited to:

- Business plans, strategies, and roadmaps
- Product ideas, concepts, and specifications
- Source code, algorithms, and technical architecture
- Customer lists, data, and usage patterns
- Financial information, pricing, and projections
- Marketing plans and competitive analysis
- Employee and contractor information
- Any information marked "Confidential" or reasonably understood to be confidential

### 1.2 Confidential Information includes:
- Written, oral, or visual disclosures
- Information disclosed in demonstrations or meetings
- Information observable from prototypes or products

---

## 2. EXCLUSIONS

### 2.1 Confidential Information does NOT include information that:

a) **Public Information**: Is or becomes publicly available through no fault of the Receiving Party

b) **Prior Knowledge**: Was already known to the Receiving Party before disclosure, as evidenced by written records

c) **Independent Development**: Is independently developed by the Receiving Party without use of or reference to the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information

d) **Third-Party Disclosure**: Is obtained from a third party who had the legal right to disclose it without restriction

e) **Written Release**: Is released from confidential treatment by written consent of the Disclosing Party

---

## 3. OBLIGATIONS

### 3.1 Protection
The Receiving Party shall:

a) Protect Confidential Information with at least the same degree of care used to protect its own confidential information, but no less than reasonable care

b) Use Confidential Information solely for the purpose of evaluating or pursuing the potential business relationship ("Permitted Purpose")

c) Limit access to Confidential Information to employees, contractors, or advisors who:
   - Have a need to know for the Permitted Purpose
   - Are bound by confidentiality obligations at least as protective as this Agreement

### 3.2 Restrictions
The Receiving Party shall NOT:

a) Disclose Confidential Information to any third party without prior written consent

b) Use Confidential Information for any purpose other than the Permitted Purpose

c) Copy or reproduce Confidential Information except as necessary for the Permitted Purpose

d) Reverse engineer, disassemble, or decompile any Confidential Information

---

## 4. COMPELLED DISCLOSURE

### 4.1 Legal Requirements
If the Receiving Party is compelled by law, regulation, or legal process to disclose Confidential Information, it shall:

a) Provide prompt written notice to the Disclosing Party (to the extent legally permitted)

b) Cooperate with the Disclosing Party's efforts to obtain protective treatment

c) Disclose only the minimum amount required

### 4.2 Continued Protection
Information disclosed pursuant to legal compulsion remains Confidential Information for all other purposes.

---

## 5. NO LICENSE OR WARRANTY

### 5.1 No License Granted
This Agreement does not grant either Party any license or rights to the other Party's Confidential Information, intellectual property, or technology, except the limited right to review for the Permitted Purpose.

### 5.2 No Warranty
ALL CONFIDENTIAL INFORMATION IS PROVIDED "AS IS." THE DISCLOSING PARTY MAKES NO WARRANTIES, EXPRESS OR IMPLIED, REGARDING ACCURACY, COMPLETENESS, OR FITNESS FOR A PARTICULAR PURPOSE.

### 5.3 No Obligation
This Agreement does not obligate either Party to:
- Enter into any business relationship
- Disclose any particular information
- Proceed with any transaction

---

## 6. RETURN OF INFORMATION

### 6.1 Upon Request or Termination
Upon written request or termination of this Agreement, the Receiving Party shall:

a) Return all tangible materials containing Confidential Information

b) Permanently delete all electronic copies

c) Certify in writing that the above has been completed

### 6.2 Exceptions
The Receiving Party may retain:
- One archival copy for legal compliance purposes
- Copies in routine backup systems (subject to continued confidentiality)

---

## 7. TERM AND TERMINATION

### 7.1 Term
This Agreement is effective from the Effective Date and continues for [2 years / duration of business discussions / specify].

### 7.2 Survival
The confidentiality obligations shall survive termination for a period of:
- **3 years** for general business information
- **5 years** for technical information and trade secrets
- **Indefinitely** for trade secrets that remain trade secrets

### 7.3 Termination
Either Party may terminate this Agreement with 30 days written notice. Termination does not affect obligations for information already disclosed.

---

## 8. REMEDIES

### 8.1 Irreparable Harm
The Parties acknowledge that breach of this Agreement may cause irreparable harm not adequately compensable by monetary damages.

### 8.2 Injunctive Relief
The Disclosing Party shall be entitled to seek injunctive relief without the need to post bond, in addition to any other legal or equitable remedies.

### 8.3 No Limitation
The pursuit of injunctive relief does not limit the Disclosing Party's right to pursue other remedies.

---

## 9. GENERAL PROVISIONS

### 9.1 Governing Law
This Agreement shall be governed by the laws of the State of Delaware, without regard to conflicts of law principles.

### 9.2 Jurisdiction
The Parties consent to exclusive jurisdiction in the state and federal courts located in Delaware.

### 9.3 Entire Agreement
This Agreement constitutes the entire agreement between the Parties regarding confidentiality and supersedes all prior discussions.

### 9.4 Amendment
This Agreement may only be modified by written agreement signed by both Parties.

### 9.5 Severability
If any provision is held unenforceable, the remaining provisions shall continue in full force.

### 9.6 No Waiver
Failure to enforce any provision shall not constitute a waiver of that provision.

### 9.7 Assignment
Neither Party may assign this Agreement without prior written consent, except to a successor in a merger or acquisition.

### 9.8 Counterparts
This Agreement may be executed in counterparts, including electronic signatures, each of which shall be deemed an original.

## APPENDIX: SIMPLIFIED ONE-WAY NDA

For situations where only one party is disclosing (e.g., showing a contractor your codebase):

```
ONE-WAY NON-DISCLOSURE AGREEMENT

This Agreement is between RR Holdings Inc. ("Discloser") and 
[Name] ("Recipient").

In exchange for access to Discloser's confidential information,
Recipient agrees:

1. To keep all disclosed information confidential
2. To use the information only for [stated purpose]
3. Not to disclose to any third party
4. To return all materials upon request
5. That this obligation lasts for 3 years

Information is NOT confidential if it:
- Is publicly known
- Recipient already knew it
- Recipient developed it independently
- A third party lawfully disclosed it

Recipient: _____________________ Date: ________
Discloser: _____________________ Date: ________
```
