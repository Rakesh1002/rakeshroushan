## 4. INTELLECTUAL PROPERTY

### 4.1 Work Product Ownership
All work product, inventions, code, designs, documentation, and materials created by Contractor in connection with Services ("Work Product") shall be the sole property of Company.

### 4.2 Work Made for Hire
To the maximum extent permitted by law, all Work Product is "work made for hire" as defined by the Copyright Act.

### 4.3 Assignment
To the extent any Work Product does not qualify as work for hire, Contractor hereby irrevocably assigns to Company all right, title, and interest in such Work Product, including all intellectual property rights.

### 4.4 Moral Rights Waiver
Contractor waives any moral rights in Work Product to the extent permitted by applicable law.

### 4.5 Pre-Existing IP
Contractor retains ownership of IP created before this Agreement ("Prior IP"). If Contractor incorporates Prior IP into Work Product:
- Contractor grants Company a perpetual, royalty-free, worldwide license to use such Prior IP
- Contractor must disclose Prior IP before incorporation

### 4.6 Open Source
Contractor shall not incorporate any open-source software into Work Product without prior written approval from Company. Upon approval, Contractor must:
- Document the specific library and license
- Ensure license compatibility with commercial use
- Include required attributions

### 4.7 Third-Party IP
Contractor warrants that Work Product will not infringe any third-party intellectual property rights.

## 5. CONFIDENTIALITY

### 5.1 Confidential Information
"Confidential Information" includes:
- Source code and algorithms
- Business plans and strategies
- Customer data and lists
- Financial information
- Technical specifications
- Any information marked confidential

### 5.2 Obligations
Contractor agrees to:
- Keep Confidential Information strictly confidential
- Use Confidential Information only for performing Services
- Not disclose to any third party without written consent
- Return or destroy upon termination

### 5.3 Exceptions
Confidential Information does not include information that:
- Is publicly available
- Was known to Contractor before disclosure
- Is independently developed without use of Confidential Information
- Is required to be disclosed by law (with notice to Company)

### 5.4 Duration
Confidentiality obligations survive termination of this Agreement for 5 years.

## 6. INDEPENDENT CONTRACTOR STATUS

### 6.1 Relationship
Contractor is an independent contractor, not an employee. This Agreement does not create:
- An employer-employee relationship
- A partnership or joint venture
- An agency relationship

### 6.2 No Benefits
Contractor is not entitled to:
- Employee benefits
- Health insurance
- Paid time off
- Retirement benefits
- Workers' compensation

### 6.3 Taxes and Insurance
Contractor is solely responsible for:
- All applicable taxes
- Social security contributions
- Health and liability insurance
- Any required business licenses

### 6.4 Control
Contractor controls the manner and means of performing Services, subject to meeting deliverables and deadlines.

## 7. REPRESENTATIONS AND WARRANTIES

### 7.1 Contractor Represents
Contractor represents and warrants that:
- They have the right to enter into this Agreement
- Services will be performed professionally
- Work Product will be original
- Work Product will not infringe third-party rights
- They will comply with applicable laws

### 7.2 No Conflicts
Contractor is not bound by any agreement that would prevent performance of Services or assignment of IP.

---

## 8. LIMITATION OF LIABILITY

### 8.1 Cap on Liability
Company's total liability under this Agreement shall not exceed the amounts paid to Contractor in the 3 months preceding the claim.

### 8.2 No Consequential Damages
Neither party shall be liable for indirect, incidental, consequential, or punitive damages.

---

## 9. INDEMNIFICATION

Contractor shall indemnify and hold harmless Company from any claims arising from:
- Contractor's breach of this Agreement
- Contractor's negligence or willful misconduct
- Claims that Work Product infringes third-party rights

## 10. NON-SOLICITATION

### 10.1 During Term
During the term of this Agreement, Contractor shall not:
- Directly solicit Company's customers for competing services
- Encourage Company's employees or contractors to leave

### 10.2 Duration
This restriction applies during the Agreement and for 12 months after termination.
