### The Daily Operating Rhythm

Each product day follows the same 3-block structure:

1. **Morning (2h): Build** ‚Äî Ship the highest-impact feature or fix the worst bug
2. **Afternoon (2h): Distribute** ‚Äî Write content, do outreach, optimize SEO, run experiments
3. **Evening (1h): Measure** ‚Äî Check analytics, read support tickets, plan tomorrow

### Sunday Ops Day

Sunday is the most important day. No building. No shipping. Just decisions:

- **Review metrics** for every product (signups, activation, retention, revenue)
- **Kill underperformers** ‚Äî if a product shows no signs of life after 2 weeks, archive it
- **Double down on winners** ‚Äî reallocate time from dead products to growing ones
- **Plan next week** ‚Äî set 3 priorities per product day

  <p class="text-sm text-text-muted leading-relaxed">The system is designed for <strong class="text-text">throughput</strong>, not perfection. A product that ships at 70% and gets user feedback beats a product that ships at 95% and nobody uses. The market is the judge, not me.</p>

## What I've Learned So Far

### Lesson 1: Speed beats perfection ‚Äî every time

The product I spent 3 months perfecting (UnQuest AI ‚Äî an AI knowledge management platform) got less traction than products I shipped in a weekend. The market doesn't care about your code quality. It cares about whether you solve a real problem.

<div class="not-prose my-8 grid md:grid-cols-2 gap-4">
  <div class="p-5 rounded-xl border border-red-500/20 bg-red-500/5">
    <p class="text-xs font-semibold text-red-400 uppercase tracking-wider mb-2">What doesn't work</p>
    <p class="text-sm text-text-muted leading-relaxed">3 months of building in isolation. Perfect architecture. Beautiful code. Zero users. Zero feedback. Zero learning.</p>
  </div>
  <div class="p-5 rounded-xl border border-green-500/20 bg-green-500/5">
    <p class="text-xs font-semibold text-green-400 uppercase tracking-wider mb-2">What works</p>
    <p class="text-sm text-text-muted leading-relaxed">1 weekend MVP. Ugly but functional. Ship to 100 people. Get 10 replies. Learn more in 48 hours than 3 months of building.</p>
  </div>
</div>

### Lesson 2: Distribution is the product

If nobody sees it, it doesn't matter how good it is. I now spend **50% of my time on go-to-market**, not building. That means:

- Writing blog posts and Twitter threads
- Launching on Product Hunt, Hacker News, Reddit
- Cold outreach to potential power users
- SEO optimization from day one
- Building in public to create a distribution flywheel

### Lesson 3: Kill fast

If a product doesn't show signs of life in 2 weeks, archive it and move on. Sentimentality is the enemy of velocity. I've killed 5 products already. Each one taught me something. None of them deserved more time.

<div class="not-prose my-8 p-5 rounded-xl border border-yellow-500/20 bg-yellow-500/5">
  <p class="text-sm font-semibold text-yellow-400 mb-2">The 2-Week Rule</p>
  <p class="text-sm text-text-muted leading-relaxed">After launch, track three signals: <strong class="text-text">organic signups</strong> (are people finding it?), <strong class="text-text">activation rate</strong> (are they using it?), and <strong class="text-text">return visits</strong> (are they coming back?). If all three are near zero after 2 weeks of active distribution, kill it.</p>
</div>

### Lesson 4: The stack is your competitive advantage

Every product in the studio runs on the same stack:

| Layer | Technology | Why |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| Compute | Cloudflare Workers | Edge-first, near-zero cold starts, global by default |
| Database | Cloudflare D1 / KV | Serverless SQL + key-value at the edge |
| Storage | Cloudflare R2 | S3-compatible, zero egress fees |
| AI | Workers AI + external APIs | Free inference for commodity models, paid for frontier |
| Frontend | Next.js / Astro | React ecosystem, SSR, great DX |
| Auth | Custom + OAuth | Keep it simple, own the user relationship |
| Payments | Stripe | Best-in-class, global coverage |

This shared infrastructure means I can spin up a new product in hours, not weeks. The boilerplate is battle-tested across 12 live products.
